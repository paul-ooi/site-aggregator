In December 2024, I wrote a [blog post outlining several accessibility certifications](../2024/why-get-certified-a-new-years-resolution.html), including [three from IAAP](https://www.accessibilityassociation.org/certification-overview "This link leaves the DubBlog website.") and the [Trusted](https://www.section508.gov/test/trusted-tester/) [Tester Process & Certification Program](https://www.section508.gov/test/trusted-tester/ "This link leaves the DubBlog website."). The post's main idea was to encourage readers to make a New Year’s resolution to earn a certification.

Recently, our own [Joanie Chembars](https://www.linkedin.com/in/joanie-chembars-75718a52/ "This link leaves the DubBlog website."), a member of the DubBot support team and author of many of our [help center articles,](https://help.dubbot.com/en/ "This link leaves the DubBlog website.") earned her Trusted Tester certification. 🎉 And even more recently, a client reached out to me about writing a post on accessibility trainings and certifications, and noted that he was currently studying to take the Trusted Tester program certification exam.

I took these as a sign and decided to take a deep dive into the Trusted Tester Process and Certification Program, exploring the training content, the exam, and the return on investment.

At its core, Trusted Tester is a [government-backed certification program](https://www.dhs.gov/trusted-tester "This link leaves the DubBlog website.") that teaches you a manual, structured approach to testing digital content for conformance with [Section 508 accessibility standards](https://www.access-board.gov/ict/ "This link leaves the DubBlog website.") . This certification is not just about theory. You learn to inspect code, use approved manual testing tools, and identify accessibility issues that automated scanners may miss. The Trusted Tester Process provides _repeatable, shareable, and reliable conformance test results._

## What Do You Learn?

The Trusted Tester certification program consists of a series of training modules, hands-on exercises, and a final certification exam. The curriculum covers accessibility basics, approved testing tools, manual test methods, and how to document results. **No formal prerequisites are required**, but familiarity with HTML and accessibility concepts is helpful.

**Note:** Although the program is Web-based and self-paced, you must complete the course within 6 months.

Here’s a sample of what is covered in the training modules.

Training begins with an introductory course that outlines Section 508 and why it matters. This course covers the legal background of the Section 508 standards, their purpose, and their role in ensuring digital accessibility. More importantly, it lays the groundwork for accessibility conformance testing, helping participants understand **why** testing exists before diving into **how** to do it.

From there, the program moves into the Section 508 Standards for Web, where the training gets more technical and hands-on. This section outlines the requirements that testers will evaluate, aligned with WCAG Level A and AA, as defined in the [Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Testing Baseline](https://ictbaseline.access-board.gov/ "This link leaves the DubBlog website."). Participants learn how to identify accessibility issues at the code and content level.

Since the Trusted Tester process is about manual testing, the next section in the training is about the two manual testing tools you will use. Those tools are the [Accessible Name and Description Inspector (ANDI)](https://section508coordinators.github.io/TrustedTester/TestEnvironment.html "This link leaves the DubBlog website.") and the [Colour Contrast Analyser](https://section508coordinators.github.io/TrustedTester/TestEnvironment.html "This link leaves the DubBlog website."). These tools are easy to use and learn, and are consistently accurate. They help testers quickly check accessibility features without digging through source code or having in-depth knowledge of programming languages.

Next are the core Trusted Tester topics - the Section 508 conformance tests. Each of these is a significant topic block in the training. This module includes 20 accessibility conformance tests. Here are just a few examples of the accessibility tests you will learn.

-   Auto-Playing and Auto-Updating Content  
    Ensure moving, animating, and / or auto-refresh areas can be paused, stopped, or hidden.  
    

-   Flashing (Flicker) Content  
    Test for flashing content that may exceed safe thresholds.  
    

-   Keyboard Access & Focus  
    Full keyboard operability, no traps, focus.  
    

-   Images  
    Alt text, decorative vs. meaningful images, special cases such as CAPTCHAs and text imagery.  
    

-   Adjustable Time Limits  
    Ensuring users can extend or turn off time constraints.  
    

-   Repetitive Content  
    Options to skip repetitive regions like navigation menus.

For the complete list of conformance tests, how to conduct them, and how to evaluate the results, read [Trusted Tester Section 508 Conformance Test Process for Web, Version 5.1.3](https://section508coordinators.github.io/SCRT/Resources/Trusted%20Tester%20Test%20Process%20v5.1.3.pdf "This link leaves the DubBlog website."). (PDF)   

Finally, there are the practice exam and the certification exam. The practice exam is a full-practice assessment that simulates the format of the final certification exam. You must pass the practice exam to receive access to the certification exam. You must score at least 85% on the certification exam, and you are given two chances to pass.🤞🏼

## What is the Timeline?

Expect to spend **40–80 hours studying and practicing** before you’re ready to pass the exam. Count on a bit more if you’re new to digital accessibility. That includes watching lessons, doing sample tests, and taking practice quizzes.

## What’s the Return on Investment?

Becoming a Trusted Tester is a worthwhile first step for anyone entering the accessibility testing field. The Trusted Tester Certification goes beyond definitions and theory. It is a practical, in-demand skill that strengthens both careers and organizations. When you become a Trusted Tester, you develop a deep understanding of accessibility and play a critical role in guiding organizations toward compliance and creating digital experiences that **everyone** can enjoy. While the certification requires a time commitment, it offers a valuable way to enhance your skills and career prospects. Most importantly, your work supports equitable access to information and services for all users.
